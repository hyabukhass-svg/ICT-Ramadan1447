Ramadan 1447 Community Message

Assalam Alaikum our dear community,

Alhamdulillah for the blessing of Islam and for allowing us to witness another Ramadan. This is our chance to pause, reflect, and reconnect with Allah (SWT). We all make mistakes, but Allah’s mercy is greater than our sins. He invites us to return to Him, and if we do so sincerely, He promises to forgive us.

Think of Ramadan as a fresh start, a time to cleanse your heart, seek forgiveness, and grow closer to Allah. Let this month be the beginning of lasting change. Most importantly, ask Allah with a sincere heart to make you among those He frees from the Fire in this blessed month.

The Quran: Your Guide This Ramadan

When we talk about Ramadan, we can’t forget the Quran, the words of Allah, sent down as a light to guide us, a healing for our hearts, and a mercy for us. This is the month in which it was revealed, so let it be more than just a book on a shelf. Open it, recite it, reflect on its meaning, and let its wisdom touch your heart.

Make the Quran your companion, and you’ll feel a difference, Ramadan will become more than just fasting; it will be a time of real connection, peace, and change.

Remembering the Ummah: We Are One Body

As you focus on yourself this Ramadan, seeking to grow and make the most of this blessed month, don’t forget the Ummah. Our beloved Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) taught us:

"The example of the believers in their love, mercy, and compassion for one another is like that of a body; when one part aches, the whole body responds with sleeplessness and fever."

You are part of this body, part of this Ummah. It’s important to feel that connection, to care for your brothers and sisters around the world, to share in their joys and their struggles. Let their pain move you, and let their strength inspire you.

Every good deed you do strengthens the Ummah, and every act of disobedience weakens it. So strive to be a source of light, mercy, and strength, not just for yourself, but for the entire Muslim community.

Supporting Our Brothers and Sisters in Need

Remember your brothers and sisters in Bangladesh, Kashmir, Palestine, Syria, Sudan, Yemen, and everywhere else in the world.

Let your heart be filled with hope and joy for the victory of the people of Gaza, celebrate their strength and pray that Allah helps them fulfill their responsibilities toward this victory, making it lasting and filled with His blessings.

At the same time, let your heart ache for the people of Sudan and all those who are suffering. Lift them up in your prayers, ask Allah to ease their hardships, protect them, and grant them steadfastness in their faith.

This Ramadan, let your love for the Ummah grow. Feel with them, pray for them, and do what you can to support them. Because when one part of the body hurts, we all feel the pain.

Give and Stand for the Truth

Don’t forget those in Gaza, Sudan, Lebanon, and Syria who are still suffering, those without shelter, food, medicine, or even the basic necessities of life. Keep them in your duas, and give whatever you can in charity.

But don’t stop at just giving, educate yourself. Learn about them, read their stories, understand their history, and stand with the truth. Be part of what is right. Caring, knowing, and acting is how we strengthen our Ummah and bring real change.

Ramadan Is Not About Overindulgence

Ramadan is not about buying excessive food or living in luxury. It’s about coming closer to Allah. Don’t let that purpose be lost in consuming more than necessary. Take only what you need, avoid waste, and remember the true goal of Ramadan.

Let it be a month of discipline, gratitude, and drawing nearer to Allah, not one of excess and distraction.

Your Family at the Islamic Center of the Triad

Your family at the Islamic Center of the Triad welcomes you with open arms. We are always grateful for your support, prayers, and the kindness you bring to our community.

This Ramadan, we’ve prepared special programs for you and your family to help you grow closer to Allah and strengthen our bond as a community. We hope you find joy and connection in them.

May Allah make this Ramadan a month of blessings, mercy, and acceptance for all of us, and may He grant us the ultimate victory, entry into Paradise and salvation from the Fire.
